Popiah

(or Fried Spring Rolls)

These fried spring rolls are a common street snack that can be found in almost every neighborhood market.

Ingredients

  • 5 40g Glass noodles, dry
  • 120g ground pork
  • 3 tsp soy sauce, divided
  • ¼ tsp black peppercorns, ground
  • ½ tsp white peppercorns, ground
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 soft shiitake mushrooms
  • 1.5 cup finely shredded cabbage
  • 1 cup grated carrots
  • 6 cilantro stems, finely chopped
  • ½ - 1 tsp salt
  • 1 Tbsp sugar
  • 2-3 Tbsp water, as needed
  • 8-inch spring roll wrappers, 12-14 pieces
  • 1 egg, beaten, for sealing the wrapper
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Sweet Chili Sauce for dipping

Instructions

  1. Soak glass noodles in room temperature water for 10 minutes. Drain, then cut into short pieces.
  2. Mix ground pork with 1 tsp of the soy sauce, set aside.
  3. In a wok or a large saute pan, add a little vegetable oil. Add garlic, black pepper, and white pepper; stir until garlic starts to turn colour slightly
  4. Add pork and cook until the pork is no longer clumpy and is almost done.
  5. Add shiitake mushrooms, noodles, cabbage, carrots, mushrooms, cilantro stems, ½ tsp salt, 2 tsp soy sauce, and sugar.
  6. Toss until noodles are fully cooked and cabbage is wilted, adding a splash of water if noodles start sticking. Remove from heat, taste, and add more salt as needed.
  7. Put the filling into a bowl and let cool before wrapping.
  8. While the filling is cooling, peel the wrappers apart so it’s easier when you wrap.
  9. Wrap individual rolls with about 1/4 cup of filling per roll.
  10. Heat 2-3 inches of oil in a pot to 350 F. Add spring rolls and fry until golden brown
  11. Take out of oil, let cool, and enjoy!
